What We Do
Canton Goodfellows provides Christmas gifts to children in Canton at Christmas time. We also provide gift cards so parents can purchase food for the holiday.
We provide this assistance to families without regard to religious preference, and all of our members — including board members — are unpaid volunteers.

Each year the program runs on a set timeline, bringing together applicant families, community sponsors, and our volunteers.
Late August
Applications Open
Online-only applications become available for Canton families to apply for Christmas assistance.
Mid-October
Application Deadline
Applications are reviewed on a first-come, first-served basis and screened against income guidelines.
Early October
Sponsor Matching Opens
Community members and organizations claim children to sponsor, purchasing toys from the child's wish list.
November 1
Families Notified
Approved families receive an email with their family number and child IDs. Save this email for Pickup Day.
Early December
Gifts Prepared
Volunteers prepare gift and food boxes. Any unsponsored children receive gifts purchased using donation funds.
December Saturday
Family Pickup Day
Approved families pick up their packages 9 AM–Noon at Canton Township Administration Building. Bring your approval email.
"Every effort was made to see that all children would receive what they needed and have at least one wish granted. It's such a noble mission."
— Canton Goodfellows volunteer
For over 70 years, Canton Goodfellows has grown from a door-to-door effort into a fully organized community institution.
1940s
The Beginning
Canton residents Edward Hauk and Robert Waldecker — volunteers with Plymouth Goodfellows — founded Canton Goodfellows. In the early years, the two men went door-to-door asking for donations.
1954
The Paper Sale
A Canton Goodfellows paper was printed and sold for donations. Money raised supported the mission of "No Child Without a Christmas."
1980s–2012
Nancy Spencer's Era
Nancy Spencer began volunteering in the mid-1980s and became president in the 1990s. She grew the program significantly, reaching schools, businesses, and the wider community. She started the Sponsor-a-Child program, which continues today.
2013–2018
Modernization Begins
John Spencer was voted president. Members joined forces to convert manual record-keeping into a computerized format, led by Natalie Anderson-Theisen.
2023
Online Applications Launch
Younger members streamlined data entry by building a new online application process. The transition was a huge success, reducing manual work significantly.
Today
Continuing the Mission
Canton Goodfellows continues to serve Canton families, powered entirely by community volunteers, with no administrative salaries — every dollar raised goes back to the children.
Board members are elected each year at the Annual Meeting in March. All members are unpaid volunteers.
